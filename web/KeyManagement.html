⚠ Legacy DSA/ElGamal key detected

This key uses DSA for signing and ElGamal for encryption — algorithms from the mid-1990s that are no longer recommended. Specific concerns:

  • Self-signatures use SHA-1, which is considered weak against collision attacks for new signatures.
  • DSA key strength is limited by the DSA parameter size (typically 1024-bit group order, equivalent to ~80-bit security).
  • ElGamal encryption is unpadded and not IND-CCA2 secure without careful implementation.

The add-in will decrypt messages sent to this key, but new messages will not be signed with it because creating new DSA/SHA-1 signatures is blocked by the underlying cryptographic library. It is strongly recommended to generate a new ECC or RSA-4096 key and share the new public key with your contacts.
